I had my final therapy session today. Well, my *second* final one. I had two therapists. I was so anxious and unsure when I arrived here in SF that I felt I needed two. I think it was a good call. I got so much from each one. Really great guys.

Anyway, this therapist (I feel weird using his name, so I'll call him "Ted") was great. He held space, pushed back, and took copious notes. And in the last session, we reviewed some of the things that stood out. Particularly, he reminded me that a couple of weeks ago I offered up a trip report from which I concluded that I had felt into a familiar "void" inside me, and that witnessing the void from its edge filled me with discomfort and terror.

I remembered the report, but the idea of there being a void there struck me as silly, now. Don't get me wrong! That was real! It really seemed like a undifferentiated hole filled my middle, where a sense of "me" should be. So what happened? Why does it feel silly now? Well, the last several weeks since then have included several "[5-7 day cycles of 'iffy' and 'freedom,'](https://huttj.substack.com/p/pulling-plugs#:~:text=5%2D7%20day%20cycles%20of%20%E2%80%9Ciffy%E2%80%9D%20and%20%E2%80%9Cfreedom%2C%E2%80%9D)" and as I've just sat in those pits and let them come (and some of them were HARD to be with), I've settled into myself in a deeper, plainer way than ever before.

I talked about this [[2026.06.21|previously]]: it seems the crowning fixation was one of connection vs freedom or "authenticity vs safety" as Ted preferred to characterize it. With that tension *accepted*, there doesn't seem to be anything to worry about or struggle for. I still have conditioning. I still have a lot of thoughts, but... they seem so much less urgent. Ted was kind enough to point out that the manifestations of all my relationships—to lovers, friends, strangers, myself, creativity, and even money—follow this very same preoccupied/disorganized attachment patterning. Fitting.

So what happened to the void?

Well, the void was a somatic experience, a felt sense, generated by what I assume was an old somatic pattern from long ago. As I felt through that, it was replaced by a very different perspective: I am not on a journey "inward" — there is nothing "in" there. Rather, I'm on a journey *through*, you might say, in the sense that the "me," the *Joshua* that I want to discover is not ready-made toothpaste in a tube, waiting to be gripped and squeezed out (eww), but rather a simple seed, in need of planting, water and sun, and *time*.

Imagine I was a crayon. "I need to find myself," I would lament, wouldn’t I? How silly that would be! Crayon Joshua, you are all there, in your totality. There's nothing cleverly hidden in your crayonness, waiting for you to melt or break yourself in two to find. Rather, the "you" that you seek is to be found *on the paper*. Pick yourself up and draw. Draw and draw and draw, and maybe, just maybe by the time you are all spent, the self you were seeking will peer back at you from the page.

Or maybe more accurately, the “you” that you seek is to be found in the very moments, in the very acts of drawing. The art is just the receipt.

The me that I'm looking for is not inside me. It's all around. Or, it will be. The void was, to my best estimation, the last vestige of my own holding back from this reality. An illusion of a hole filled with nothing, held apart where no one could get to it, waiting for me to stop hiding the idea of an undiscovered self from myself.

How funny!

I wonder if anyone else has found themselves in a similar predicament? Is this a common occurrence? Do many people find themselves playing a fake game of hide and seek with their true nature? Or was it just me?

Funnily enough, a few small things have been instrumental in helping me see this inversion.

I’ve been making little art pieces and realizing the fulfillment I’ve sought was in my hands the whole time. Some of my oldest pieces are continual sources of pride and satisfaction. New ones are similarly delights. Somehow I didn’t notice this before? Or I thought it was too small or not enough? What?

I realized that I could tune into myself and tune the world out at will, and it’s actually quite a soothing and relaxing practice. (Subbing out the preoccupied attachment—aka fixating on others and the environment—with something a bit different.) Ironically, Ted suggested this might be dissociation. I thought that hyperfixating on the environment and ignoring my body was more akin to dissociation. Maybe either can be! 🙃

I’ve been taking my sweet ass time doing this “research” and writing my findings. My mind is telling me that I should do more. It’s telling me that I need to be more productive. My mind is tuned for a Joshua with a different set of priorities, and I realize that now. It will get in line...

I acknowledged with Ted that I know what I want to do. I want to live and love without attachment—openly, deeply, and honestly—but no clinging. I want to create, experiment, and explore. I’m not sure the fixated, exploitative mind is something that I want to engage, from here on out. Maybe. Maybe I’m doing it now, slightly. But I certainly don’t want it in overdrive, as it has been.

I wonder what that’ll be like.

Oh, and Ted helped me see how my standard approach to connection is maximum Joshua or maximum attunement. No wonder I burn people out!
