I met up with a friend yesterday and posed the question to him. It's the first time I really posed it to anyone. "Why are you alive?"

What I noticed, and I shouldn't be surprised, is that the conversation quickly shifted to center around the nature of his current struggle. He's at a crossroads, he explained, and he went on to describe a lot of the day-to-day pulls that he is contending with. I got the sense that his focus is on the very immediate and very present orientation to his current situation.

I imagine a lot of us are like that.

What else could we answer?

I imagine a visible few of us will talk about an object level thing we love. Writing, running, skiing, etc. Kids. Teaching. Maybe we've found a hobby or a vocation and in it we find ourselves sufficiently lit up that it becomes a driving force in our lives. I'll call those the "object people." They can explain what they love to someone in a 2 minute conversation.

Some of us will have a larger mission or vision for what we want to do or achieve. It may or may not be characterized by a clear, measurable goal. Women's rights, climate change, criminal justice reform, etc. These are the "movement people." They're focused on changing some structural aspect of our reality.
