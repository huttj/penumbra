The "big crunch." The short of it is, we're overworked, overstressed, and undersexed. Not all of us, and not everywhere, but enough of us that it seems like a problem! What is going on?

Dr. K says it's an attention issue.

![[fundamental attentional loss.mov]]

> So what we're sort of seeing is like addiction to technology because there is a weakening of our attentional capability. And then if we're not using the technology, we're suffering. And the only antidote we have is this. So we turn to this again and then we get weaker and weaker and weaker. And we're really hitting like a critical state here, which is why we have like, you know, mental health, epidemiology, there's like global pandemics of loneliness, suicidality, anxiety, depression, all these things are getting really bad. And I think it's due to this fundamental attentional loss.

From [*The Neuroscience of Mystical Experiences Your Brain is Capable Of | Dr. K*](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=YgkQTGotufk)

That checks out. I'm not actually sure the crunch can be solved purely systemically. My gut tells me it can—whatever produced us could produce something else; all things are possible, but the configuration space is sparse and often opaque. Either way, though, we can adjust our own attentional mechanisms. I've done some work to this end, and I can see myself doing a lot more. It's clear to me, based on the meager amount I've done so far, that our attentional habits *are* emotional state management habits. Kind of like Dr. K alludes to in the quote, "We're suffering, and the only antidote we have is this."

I'm also reminded of Gabor Mate's take. He claims it's not a disease and that it's not heritable. I'm not sure I'm willing to go that far. My own instinct says that a lot of similar affects in our psychology are achievable through multiple pathways. I'm inclined to believe that two very different brains can present in very similar ways. Maybe one is trauma and another is a unique chemistry that is genetically encoded. It stands to reason, at least. Dr. Rege [presents a similar view](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=c4WOTmPBoLE).

Whichever the case may be, it behooves us, if we have the luxury to do so, to examine our emotional conditioning and habits of attention, and try to feel our way to our centers, first. I think it would be a shame to treat as genetic something that is entirely, or largely, conditioned! What opportunities we'd miss, taking painkillers to run when we just needed bigger shoes with better arch support! At the same time, I'd be remiss to suggest that any particular person abstain from medication. If you feel you need it, you probably do. If you're inclined to try without it, try!

I suspect the path to global decrunching will necessarily be buffeted by individuals taking their attention and emotional experiences into their own hands, fully and completely. Surely we can change the systems to facilitate looser reigns on our collective psyches, but the individual strategy is a central element in my view, in either case. We must wiggle free, perhaps first, so that we can loosen the cultural knots that bind us.
